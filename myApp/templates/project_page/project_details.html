THE PROJECT

A Landscape
Worthy of Legacy

The Burrawang Estate commission began with a deceptively simple brief: make the grounds feel as extraordinary as the house. The 110-year-old homestead sat within 8 hectares of paddock, overgrown gardens and remnant bushland — beautiful in potential, neglected in reality.

Our design director spent three days on site before a single sketch was made — walking the land at different times of day, studying light, tracing historic garden walls half-buried in the soil, and listening to the clients talk about the generations they hoped would enjoy what we created.

The result is a landscape designed for time — structured enough to feel considered, natural enough to feel inevitable, and resilient enough to improve with every passing year.

THE STORY

Challenge & Solution

THE CHALLENGES
01
Degraded soil across the entire site

Decades of cattle grazing had compacted the soil to near-clay density. Nothing ornamental could establish without a complete remediation programme across 8 hectares.

02
No existing water infrastructure

The site had one bore pump and no reticulation. A full irrigation network serving 14 separate zones needed to be engineered from scratch and buried before any planting began.

03
Heritage walls requiring careful restoration

Original dry-stone walls from the 1890s were partially collapsed. Restoration required a specialist stone mason working alongside our team for six weeks.

OUR SOLUTIONS
01
Deep ripping + custom soil blend

We deep-ripped the entire site to 600mm, imported 1,200 cubic metres of custom-blended compost, and established a mycorrhizal inoculation programme that accelerated plant establishment by an estimated 40%.

02
Smart 14-zone irrigation network

We engineered a gravity-fed system from a new 250,000-litre header tank, supplemented by rainwater harvesting from all buildings. Smart controllers adjust schedules in real-time based on rainfall data.

03
Stone-by-stone heritage restoration

Every stone was catalogued, cleaned and re-laid in its original position by our specialist team. The restored walls now form the primary structure of the formal garden's four-quadrant layout.

HOW WE BUILT IT

The Process

PHASE 01
SEPT – OCT 2022 · 8 WEEKS

Discovery & Masterplan

Three site visits over eight weeks allowed us to map soil conditions, drainage patterns, sun angles at every season, and the hidden archaeology of old garden walls. The masterplan document — 47 pages — became the project's bible, consulted daily throughout the build.

PHASE 02
NOV 2022 – FEB 2023 · 16 WEEKS

Earthworks & Infrastructure

The entire site was deep-ripped and reprofiled. New drainage channels, 6.2km of irrigation pipe, the header tank system, and all electrical conduit for lighting were installed and backfilled before any planting commenced. Getting this foundation right was non-negotiable.

PHASE 03
MAR – MAY 2023 · 12 WEEKS

Hardscape & Heritage Restoration

The heritage stone walls were painstakingly restored — 340 linear metres in total. New stone pathways were laid in a herringbone pattern using reclaimed sandstone sourced from a demolished Sydney building. The formal garden's central fountain was cast by a local artisan stonemason.

PHASE 04
JUNE – SEPT 2023 · 18 WEEKS

Planting & Establishment

Over 2,400 individual plants were installed across 11 distinct character zones — from the clipped formality of the parterre garden to the naturalistic wildflower meadow and the mixed woodland belt. Every specimen tree was positioned to the centimetre, guided by 3D sun-path modelling.

PHASE 05
OCT – NOV 2023 · 6 WEEKS

Lighting, Finishing & Handover

The final phase saw 186 individual light fixtures installed — from mushroom bollards along the woodland walk to dramatic uplighting on specimen trees and subtle wash lighting on the restored stone walls. The handover included a comprehensive care manual and a 12-month aftercare programme.

THE OUTCOME

Numbers That
Tell the Story

Two years after handover, the estate has been recognised by two industry bodies, featured in three national publications, and — most importantly — has become the backdrop for family life in exactly the way our clients imagined.
